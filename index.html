About Nicole Choiniere-Kroeker

After twenty-six years as an educator I have decided to follow a new career path in the computer programming world. My first start in coding was back in the 1990s when education was in the infant stages of getting connected. Another teacher and I started working with HTML to craft the school's first website. My interest in technology as a tool in education continued to grow and I earned my first Master's degree in Technology Education. I became a leader in incorporating tech tools in the classroom. My second Master's degree in school-library media and my National Board Certified Teacher portfolio offered more opportunities to explore this path.

As an educator, I honed my skills in identifying challenges and developing solutions. I expressed my creativity with videos, websites, and interactive tech tools. I sought the answers to questions and each year I taught myself how to use resources to improve my teaching and my students' experiences. This skillset is part of being a strong programmer. My motto is to keep learning and growing.

I have many interests to keep me busy. I like to bake and I've taken classes at the King Arthur Baking School in Vermont. I knit, make quilts, read and write stories. I enjoy playing golf, paddle boarding and learning to row.

I enjoy visiting new places. In the projects section you will see images of places I've been. I used them as buttons for my projects.
